 Forms

Form SG.39:

Protocol and Liaison Service Focal Point Designation Form

This form is the first step to obtaining many documents and forms. This form is to confirm the person designated to handle accreditation matters. It must be completed, signed by the Mission’s Permanent Representative, and returned to the Protocol and Liaison Service Office. It is worth noting that the form may not be altered in any way if it is to be recognized and processed by the Office.

Form SG.5:

Mission Registration Form for Protocol and Liaison Services Office

This form provides a platform for which Missions must register on to be recognized by the Protocol and Liaison Services Office. The form must be filled out completely and then returned to the Office. It is worth noting that the form may not be altered in any way if it is to be recognized and processed by the Office.

Form SG.32:

Grounds Pass Registration Form for Missions and Permanent Observer Offices

This form is the basic grounds pass registration form for Missions and Permanent Observer Offices for when new delegates rotate into these respective offices. This form must be filled out in its entirety and returned to the Protocol and Liaison Services Office. It is worth noting that the form may not be altered in any way if it is to be recognized and processed by the Office.

Form SG.38:

Ground passes for former Permanent Representatives of Mission Offices

This form is similar to SG-32, except it specifically addresses obtaining ground passes for former Permanent Representatives of Mission Offices. It is worth noting that the form may not be altered in any way if it is to be recognized and processed by the Office.

Form SG.8:

Notification of End of Duty of Members of Permanent Missions or Observer Offices to the United Nations.”

This form is officially titled the “Notification of End of Duty of Members of Permanent Missions or Observer Offices to the United Nations.” It notifies the Protocol and Liaison Services Office that certain delegates are no longer in rotation at their respective missions at the United Nations. It is worth noting that the form may not be altered in any way if it is to be recognized and processed by the Office.

Security Letter Form:

Participation without Right to Vote.

This form enables delegations to participate in the Security Council without the right to vote, in order to co- sponsor a draft resolution.
